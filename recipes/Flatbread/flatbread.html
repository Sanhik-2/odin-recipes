  • Aish Flat Bread

    • Aish Baladi:Aish Baladi is an ancient Egyptian Flatbread made with 100% Whole Wheat flour and is a Vegan flatbread.This Vegan Egyptian Flatbread looks and feels very similar to Pita, but it tastes very different. It forms a great pocket just like pita bread to stuff literally anything. All we needed was a good smear of hummus, and it was heavenly

    Here's the recipe:

    Ingredients:

    1. 2 cups whole wheat flour
    2. 1 cup all-purpose flour
    3. 1 1/2 tsp salt
    4. 1 tbsp olive oil
    5. 1 cup warm water

    Instructions:

    1. In a large bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, all-purpose flour, and salt.
    2. Add the olive oil and warm water to the dry ingredients. Mix until a dough forms.
    3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
    4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rest for 1 hour.
    5. Preheat a skillet or griddle over medium-high heat.
    6. Divide the dough into 8 equal portions and roll each portion into a ball.
    7. Roll out each ball into a thin circle, about 6-8 inches in diameter.
    8. Cook each flatbread on the preheated skillet for about 1-2 minutes on each side, or until puffed and golden brown spots appear.
    9. Remove from the skillet and keep warm in a clean towel. Repeat with the remaining dough.
    10. Serve warm with your favorite dips or fillings.

  • Dhal Puri

    • Dhal Puri is a type of flatbread originating from the Indian subcontinent, particularly popular in Trinidad and Tobago, Guyana, and Suriname. It is made from a combination of yellow split peas (dhal) and wheat flour, resulting in a flavorful and hearty bread. Dhal Puri is typically served with various curries, stews, or chutneys and is a staple in Indo-Caribbean cuisine.

    Here's the recipe:

      Ingredients:

      1. 1 cup yellow split peas (dhal), soaked for at least 2 hours
      2. 2 cups all-purpose flour
      3. 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
      4. 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
      5. 1/2 tsp salt
      6. 1 tbsp vegetable oil
      7. 3/4 cup warm water (adjust as needed)

      Instructions:

      1. Drain the soaked yellow split peas and rinse them well.
      2. In a food processor, combine the split peas, turmeric powder, cumin seeds, and salt. Pulse until you get a coarse mixture. Set aside.
      3. In a large bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt. Add the vegetable oil and warm water. Mix until a dough forms.
      4. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
      5. Divide the dough into 8 equal portions and roll each portion into a ball.
      6. Roll out each ball into a thin circle, about 6-8 inches in diameter.
      7. Spoon a portion of the split pea mixture onto the center of each rolled-out dough circle. Fold the edges over the filling and pinch to seal.
      8. Gently roll out the filled dough again into a flat circle, being careful not to tear it.
      9. Heat a skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. Cook each Dhal Puri for about 1-2 minutes on each side, or until golden brown spots appear.
      10. Remove from the skillet and keep warm in a clean towel. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
      11. Serve warm with your favorite curries or chutneys.

    1. Paratha

      2. Paratha is a type of Indian flatbread that is commonly consumed in South Asia. It is made from whole wheat flour and is known for its flaky and layered texture. Parathas can be plain or stuffed with various fillings such as potatoes, paneer, or vegetables. They are typically cooked on a griddle with ghee or oil, resulting in a crispy exterior and a soft interior. Parathas are often enjoyed with yogurt, pickles, or curries and are a popular choice for breakfast or any meal of the day.

      Here's the recipe:

      Ingredients:

        1. 2 cups whole wheat flour
        2. 1/2 tsp salt
        3. 3/4 cup water (adjust as needed)
        4. 2 tbsp ghee or oil (for cooking)

        Instructions:

        1. In a large bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and salt. Gradually add water and mix until a dough forms.
        2. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
        3. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rest for 30 minutes.
        4. Divide the dough into 6 equal portions and roll each portion into a ball.
        5. Roll out each ball into a thin circle, about 6-8 inches in diameter.
        6. Heat a skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. Cook each paratha for about 1-2 minutes on each side, applying ghee or oil as needed, until golden brown spots appear.
        7. Remove from the skillet and keep warm in a clean towel. Repeat with the remaining dough.
        8. Serve warm with yogurt, pickles, or curries.
